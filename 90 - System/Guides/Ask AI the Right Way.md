# 💬 Ask AI the Right Way

Most people talk to AI like a search box. They type five words and take whatever comes back. Then they wonder why it is generic, or wrong, and why they did not actually learn anything. The problem is not the AI. It is the ask.

Here is the thing: **AI is not a spectator sport.** It speeds up learning, but you still have to read, think, and do the reps. Used right, it is the best tutor you will ever have, available at midnight, endlessly patient. Used wrong, it does your thinking for you and you get dumber while feeling smarter. The difference is entirely in how you ask.

## The three pillars of a good ask

Give it three things and the quality jumps immediately.

1. **Role.** Tell it who to be. "You are a patient calculus tutor." "You are a strict English teacher." The role sets the whole tone.
2. **Context.** Tell it the situation and what you already tried. "We are on quadratics. Here is the problem. Here is my attempt. I get stuck at this step." Context is what makes the answer about you instead of about everyone.
3. **Task.** Tell it exactly what you want, and make it the honest version. Not "solve this," but "explain the step I am missing so I can finish it myself."

Weak ask: "solve x squared plus 5x plus 6 equals 0."
Strong ask: "You are a patient math tutor. We are factoring quadratics. I got to (x plus 2)(x plus 3) but I do not understand where the 2 and 3 come from. Explain just that part, then give me a similar one to try."

One of those does the problem for you. The other one teaches you to do the next ten.

## The rule that keeps it honest

**AI proposes. You decide. You are the editor.**

Let it draft, explain, suggest, and quiz. You make every real call, and you check its work. Because it will be confident and it will still be wrong sometimes. It makes up facts, botches math, invents quotes. Your job is to know enough to catch it.

So: **always verify.** If it states a fact, ask "where does that come from," or check it against your notes or the textbook. Make it show its receipts. The whole skill is being able to tell when the tool is lying to you, and you only get that by staying in the driver's seat.

## Use it like a tutor, not a vending machine

- "Quiz me on these notes, one question at a time, and do not show the answer until I try." (This is studying. See [Study with AI](Study%20with%20AI%20(NotebookLM%20and%20Gemini).md).)
- "I think the answer is X because Y. Poke holes in my reasoning."
- "Explain this like I am fourteen, then check if I understood by asking me a question."
- "Grade my draft against this rubric, harshest critic possible, problems first." (This is reviewing. See [Be Your Own Reviewer](Be%20Your%20Own%20Reviewer.md).)

Notice what none of those say: "do it for me." That is the line. Ask it to make you sharper, never to hand you the answer you were supposed to earn.
