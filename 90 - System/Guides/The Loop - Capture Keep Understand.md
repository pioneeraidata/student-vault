# 🔄 The Loop: Capture, Keep, Understand

This vault is built around one idea. In a world where AI can hand you an answer to almost anything, the edge is not knowing the answer. The machine has answers. The edge is three habits:

> **Get your thoughts out fast. Keep what matters. Actually understand it instead of taking the machine's word for it.**

Capture. Keep. Understand. One tool for each.

## 1. Capture: say what you mean, fast

The kid who can talk through an idea out loud, clearly, runs circles around the one staring at a blank page. Your voice is the fastest interface you own.

A voice to text tool (Wispr Flow is a good one) turns talking into typed text anywhere. Blank page for an essay? Talk it out loud, get the mess onto the screen, then shape it. The point is not the app, it is the habit: **get the thought out of your head and into words before it evaporates.** Your [daily note](../../01%20-%20Daily/About%20Daily%20Notes.md) is where most capture lands.

## 2. Keep: own your memory

This vault is the Keep step. A notebook you own, forever, in plain text, on your machine, not trapped in someone else's app. Every idea linked to the next.

It teaches you to keep your own memory and connect your own dots, instead of renting your brain to whatever app is popular this year. That pile of notes becomes the raw material for everything else: essays, study guides, college applications, ideas. You are not taking notes to file them. You are building a memory you can search and connect.

## 3. Understand: make the machine show its receipts

This is the habit that matters most, and the one most people skip.

Do not just search a question and swallow the top answer. Tools like NotebookLM let you feed in **your own sources**: your notes, a textbook, a stack of PDFs. Then it answers your questions from those sources, with a citation for every claim, so you can check the work.

The move is: work from real sources, ask the hard question, and make the machine show its receipts. Point it at your class notes and your reading and ask it to quiz you, or to explain the part you did not follow, using only that material. That turns a pile of stuff into actual understanding, instead of a confident answer you cannot verify.

## The loop

Put them together and you get a loop:

> Talk it out. Keep it. Then question it until you truly understand it.

Do that and you own your thinking, in a world full of people who outsourced theirs. That is the whole reason this vault exists. Not to do the work for you. To make you the kind of person who is ten times more useful with these tools in hand.
