# 🔍 Be Your Own Reviewer

Here is a rule the pros live by and most students never hear: **the person who made the work should not be the only person who checks it.** In a real company, the person who writes something does not get to be the only one who approves it. Fresh eyes catch what tired eyes wrote. You can use that same trick on your own homework, even working alone.

## Why you cannot trust your first pass

Right after you finish something, you are the worst person to judge it. You know what you meant, so you read what you meant, not what is actually on the page. Your brain fills the gaps. That is why you turn in the essay with the missing word and only see it after it is graded.

So you split yourself into two people. **Writer you** makes the thing. **Reviewer you** tears it apart. The catch is they cannot be the same person at the same moment.

## Three ways to get fresh eyes

1. **Time.** Finish the night before, review in the morning. Even one hour helps. Sleep resets your brain enough to read it like a stranger would.
2. **A swap.** Trade with a friend. You check theirs, they check yours. You will both spot things the author went blind to.
3. **An AI reviewer, used right.** Paste your own draft and say "you are a strict teacher grading this against the rubric, find every weakness before you say anything nice." That is using AI as a critic, not a ghostwriter. It finds problems. You decide what to fix. See [Ask AI the Right Way](Ask%20AI%20the%20Right%20Way.md).

## Review like a pro: problems first

When you review, do not start with what you like. Start hunting for what is broken, worst first. Real reviewers list the critical problems before the small ones, and they never soften it. Be that harsh with your own work, because the teacher will be.

Run down this order:

- **Critical:** Does it actually answer the question that was asked? Miss this and nothing else matters.
- **High:** Is every claim backed by evidence? Any missing step, any fact you cannot source?
- **Medium:** Is it clear? Would someone who is not you follow it?
- **Low:** Spelling, formatting, the small stuff. Last, not first.

## The quality gate

Here is the part that changes everything. **"Done" is not when you are tired of it. Done is when it passes the check.** Set the bar before you start (see [Do Work You're Proud Of](Do%20Work%20You're%20Proud%20Of.md)), then do not call it finished until it clears the bar. Being sick of an assignment is not the same as the assignment being good.

Two people, one of you. Writer, then reviewer, then done. That is how you stop turning in work with holes you could have caught.
