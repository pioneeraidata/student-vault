# 🤖 Study with AI (NotebookLM and Gemini)

Your school probably blocks ChatGPT and Claude. Here is the thing most people miss: **NotebookLM and Gemini are Google tools.** If your school runs on Google (most do), they often ride in through the same account that runs your email and Docs, while the other AI tools stay blocked. And even off the school network, both have free versions.

The bigger reason this works: your Obsidian notes are **plain text**. That means they feed into any AI cleanly, with no exporting, no weird formats, no lock-in. You own the notes, so you can point any tool at them. That is the whole advantage of keeping your brain in plain text instead of trapped in an app.

## First, the honest-work line

Using AI to **quiz you on your own notes** is studying. It is the same as a friend holding your flashcards. Asking AI to **write your essay** is cheating, and teachers can tell. This guide is about the first thing. Work from your own sources, make the tool show its receipts, and you learn faster without crossing any line. See [The Loop](The%20Loop%20-%20Capture%20Keep%20Understand.md).

## NotebookLM (the study buddy that cites your notes)

Free at **notebooklm.google.com**. This is the better of the two for studying, because it only answers from the sources *you* give it, and it puts a citation on every claim so you can check the work against your own notes.

### Set it up (5 minutes before a test)

1. Go to notebooklm.google.com and click **New Notebook**. Make one per test or per unit (example: "Bio Unit 3").
2. Add your notes as sources. Three ways, pick whatever is easiest:
   - **Copy and paste (always works):** open a note in Obsidian, select all, copy, then in NotebookLM click **Add source**, choose **Paste text**, paste.
   - **Drag the file in:** drag the `.md` note straight into the sources panel. If it will not take the `.md` ending, rename a copy to `.txt` first (it is the same plain text).
   - **From Google Drive:** if your vault syncs to Drive (see below), pick the files right from there.
3. Add every note for that unit: your class notes, your study guide, even the reading notes.

### What to ask it

- "Quiz me on these notes. One question at a time. Do not show the answer until I try."
- "What are the most important ideas here, and which ones am I most likely to be tested on?"
- "Explain [the thing you did not follow in class] using only these notes, simply."
- "Make a study guide from these sources."
- Click **Audio Overview** and it turns your notes into a two-person podcast you can listen to on the way to school. This is a real cheat code for review.

Every answer links back to the exact note it came from. That is the receipts habit. If it says something that is not in your sources, you will see it has nothing to cite, and you will know to double check.

## Gemini (the fast one)

At **gemini.google.com**. If your school uses Google Workspace, you may already have access through your school account. Otherwise a personal Google account works. Gemini is faster and more general than NotebookLM, but it does not stay locked to your sources, so you have to keep it honest yourself.

### The fastest path

1. Open your note in Obsidian, select all, copy.
2. Go to gemini.google.com, paste your notes into the message box.
3. Ask: "Here are my class notes. Quiz me on them one question at a time, wait for my answer, then tell me if I am right and cite the part of my notes." Or: "Explain the part about X in plain language, then give me three practice questions."

### If your vault is in Google Drive

Gemini can reach into your Google Drive. If you sync the vault folder to Drive (below), you can say "use my notes in the [folder] and quiz me," and keep everything in one place.

## Getting your Obsidian notes into Google

- **Copy and paste** is the path that never fails. For one test's worth of notes it takes a minute.
- **Sync the whole vault to Google Drive:** put the `StudentVault` folder inside your Google Drive folder on your computer. Now every note is in Drive automatically, ready for NotebookLM or Gemini, and backed up. See [Setup](Setup%20-%20Turn%20On%20These%20Plugins.md).
- **`.md` is just text.** If any tool refuses the `.md` ending, make a copy and rename it to `.txt`. Nothing else changes.

## The one rule

These tools work from **your** sources. That is the point. Do not ask them for an answer you cannot check. Ask them to quiz you, find your gaps, and explain the hard part from the notes you already took. That is the difference between a tool that makes you smarter and one that does your thinking for you.
