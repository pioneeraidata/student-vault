# 🧠 Study Smarter

Most studying is a waste of time. Not because students are lazy, but because the popular methods barely work. Here is what actually does, and how this vault is built for it.

## The thing that does not work: rereading

Reading your notes over and over feels productive. It is not. It makes the material feel familiar, and your brain confuses "familiar" with "I know this." Then the test asks you to produce the answer and it is not there. Highlighting is the same trap. It feels like studying and teaches you almost nothing.

## The thing that works: active recall

Active recall means **pulling the answer out of your head before you check it.** Close the notes. Ask yourself the question. Struggle to remember. Then look.

That struggle is the whole point. The effort of retrieving something is what wires it in. Easy studying builds weak memory. A little difficulty builds strong memory. This is the single most proven finding in the science of learning, and almost nobody uses it.

In this vault: the [Active Recall template](../Templates/Active%20Recall.md) and building a [Study Guide](../Templates/Study%20Guide.md) in your own words are both active recall. Writing the guide without looking is studying. Quizzing yourself is studying. Rereading the guide is not.

## The second thing that works: spacing it out

Studying five days for twenty minutes beats one night for a hundred minutes, even though it is the same total time. Every time you almost forget something and then pull it back, the memory gets stronger. Cramming skips that, which is why crammed material is gone by the weekend.

So: make the study guide early, then run the recall deck a little each day up to the test. Only redo the questions you miss.

## Use AI the right way here

Point an AI that reads your own sources (see [The Loop](The%20Loop%20-%20Capture%20Keep%20Understand.md)) at your class notes and say "quiz me on this, one question at a time, and do not show the answer until I try." Now the machine is running your active recall for you, from your real material, and you can check every answer against the source. That is AI making you smarter instead of doing the thinking for you.

## The test-week routine

1. Five days out: build the study guide from your class notes, in your own words.
2. Each day after: run the recall deck. Redo only the misses.
3. Night before: light review, then sleep. Sleep is when memory locks in. An all-nighter trades the grade you want for the grade you get.
