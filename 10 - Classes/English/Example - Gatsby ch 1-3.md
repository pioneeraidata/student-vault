# 📖 English: The Great Gatsby, Chapters 1 to 3

> This is an example note so you can see the pattern. Make one of these with the Class Note template for each lecture. Delete this once you have the idea.

**Class:** [[English MOC]]
**Date:** 2026-01-14

## What we covered

- Narrator is Nick Carraway. He is telling the story after the fact, so he is not neutral. Watch for where he judges people while claiming he does not.
- Setting: East Egg (old money) vs West Egg (new money). The geography is a status map.
- Gatsby throws huge parties but barely appears at them. He is watching for one person.

## Key quotes

- "I am inclined to reserve all judgments." Nick, chapter 1. Ironic, because he judges everyone.
- The green light at the end of Daisy's dock. First appearance of the book's biggest symbol.

## Questions I still have

- Is Nick a reliable narrator? Teacher hinted this is the whole essay topic.
- What does the green light actually stand for? Money, Daisy, the future, all three?

## Understand it (do not just reread)

Explain the East Egg vs West Egg split to yourself in one sentence, out loud. If you can, you get it. If you fumble, that is the part to ask about tomorrow.
