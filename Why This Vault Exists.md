# Why This Vault Exists

AI can hand you an answer to almost anything. That is exactly why knowing the answer is not the edge anymore. The machine already has the answers. The edge is being the kind of person these tools make ten times more useful, instead of ten times lazier.

So it comes down to three habits.

**Capture.** Get the thought out of your head before it evaporates. Your voice is the fastest tool you own, so talk it out.

**Keep.** Put it somewhere you own forever, in plain text, on your machine. Not rented from whatever app is popular this year.

**Understand.** Do not swallow the top answer. Work from your own sources, ask the hard question, and make the machine show its receipts.

Talk it out. Keep it. Then question it until you actually understand it. Do that and you own your thinking, in a world full of people who outsourced theirs.

None of this is about doing the work for you. It is about doing real work, keeping your promises to yourself, and stacking good days on top of each other. That is the whole point.

I built this for my own kid, then figured other people could use it too. Make it yours.

## How this vault is different

This is not just folders. It is a way of working, pulled from how real teams do quality work:

- **Be your own reviewer.** The person who made the work should not be the only one who checks it. Get fresh eyes before it counts. See [Be Your Own Reviewer](90%20-%20System/Guides/Be%20Your%20Own%20Reviewer.md).
- **Write the rubric first.** You cannot judge work if you never set a bar for it. See [Do Work You're Proud Of](90%20-%20System/Guides/Do%20Work%20You're%20Proud%20Of.md).
- **AI proposes, you decide, you are the editor.** Use it as a tutor and a critic, never a ghostwriter. See [Ask AI the Right Way](90%20-%20System/Guides/Ask%20AI%20the%20Right%20Way.md).
- **Try the simple thing first. Fix the foundation, not the polish.** See [How to Think](90%20-%20System/Guides/How%20to%20Think.md).
