# ⏳ My Commitments

Your fixed weekly schedule in one place. The stuff that repeats: school hours, practice, music lessons, games, a job, church, whatever holds a standing spot. Once this is written down, you can actually see how much free time you really have, which is almost always less than it feels like.

Update it when a season starts or ends, because that is when your whole week changes shape.

## My fixed week

| Day | Committed | Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | School, Soccer practice | 8-3, 3:30-5:30 |
| Tue | School, Music lesson | 8-3, 6:00-6:45 |
| Wed | School, Soccer practice | 8-3, 3:30-5:30 |
| Thu | School, Soccer practice | 8-3, 3:30-5:30 |
| Fri | School | 8-3 |
| Sat | Game (most weeks) | varies |
| Sun | Rest / homework / plan the week | |

> Replace the examples with your real week.

## What this leaves me

Add up the open hours after school and on weekends. That number, minus sleep and travel, is what homework, studying, friends, and downtime all share. Seeing it is the first step to spending it on purpose instead of wondering where it went.

## When a season is on

In season, practice and games swallow your afternoons. That is the hardest time to keep grades and sleep steady, so it is exactly when you plan hardest. Build each week with the [Weekly Planner](../90%20-%20System/Templates/Weekly%20Planner.md) and read [Managing Your Time](../90%20-%20System/Guides/Managing%20Your%20Time.md).
