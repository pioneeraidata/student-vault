# 🤝 Friends & Social Life

Your social life is not a distraction from the important stuff. It is some of the important stuff. The friendships you build now are part of what high school is actually for, and no transcript ever made anyone happy by itself. This note is not about tracking people like a database. It is a light place to be intentional, so friends do not quietly get squeezed out by a busy season, and so a busy social stretch does not quietly wreck your grades. Both directions matter.

## The balance, honestly

Two failure modes, and you have probably seen both:

- **Friends crowd out everything.** Every night is a hangout, homework slides, sleep disappears, grades follow. Fun now, rough later.
- **Everything crowds out friends.** All school and sport and practice, no people, and you burn out and feel alone even though you are "doing everything right."

The fix is the same for both: put social time on the [Weekly Planner](../90%20-%20System/Templates/Weekly%20Planner.md) on purpose. A real block, chosen by you, not whatever is left over and not everything by default. When you plan it, you get to enjoy it without the guilt, because the homework is already handled.

## When it gets hard to say no

You will get invited to things during a test week or a busy stretch. Saying no to one is not being a bad friend, it is being a person with priorities, and real friends get it. Say yes to the ones that matter, move on from the rest, and do not let a fear of missing out run your whole schedule.
